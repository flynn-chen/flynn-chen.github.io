Physical Activity Guidelines

It's not always easy to fit in physical activity daily! Here are some key guidelines:

  1. Try to sit less and move more throughout the day
    • Even if this is parking farther in a parking lot, taking a brisk walk during lunch, scheduling a five-minute break every hour or two to take a lap around the office, walking in place or stretching while watching your favorite show—anything helps.
    • One scientifically proven goal—aim for 10,000 steps a day.
  2. For substantial health benefits:
    • Aim for 150-300 minutes of moderate intensity activity
    • These are activities where you can talk, but not sing while doing the activity. Examples include walking briskly, heavy cleaning like vacuuming or mopping, mowing lawn, bicycling with light effort, badminton, tennis doubles, pickleball OR
    • Aim for 75-150 minutes of vigorous intensity activity
    • These are activities where you cannot say more than a few words without pausing for a breath. Examples include hiking, jogging, shoveling, carrying heavy loads, bicycling fast, basketball game, soccer, tennis singles
  3. Aim for muscle strengthening activities of moderate/great intensity 2 or more days/week
    • Examples include lifting weights, gardening, climbing stairs, cycling, dance, yoga, push-ups/squats/sit-ups, carrying groceries
  4. Older adults—be as active as your condition allows. Try to incorporate balance exercises in addition to these activities above.
